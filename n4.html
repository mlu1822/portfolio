N-4

Designed an e-bike specifically tailored for campus and city patrollers, supporting all the daily tasks that law enforcements handle while relieving some pain points they have when riding out-of-box bikes

Background

Bike patrol units excel at approaching in a non-enforcer manner and maneuvering in tight spaces where a patrol car has a disadvantage in. This allows bike patrol officers to be more personal than other units.

However, current police bikes are not fitted for their rigorous routine, affecting their work and community presence. Controls do not comply to high velocity human factor principles, and they are not perceived as friendly as they wish.

Approach

The main challenge was creating a bike that was an extension of the police officer and not just another transportation method. To tackle this, we implemented features to assist daily tasks, providing speed and comfort.

In the process, we reduced the amount of pain points police officers were encountering due to misrepresented gear.

Impact

After completing the project, Kevin Shankwiler↗ reached out to inform me that our bike is in consideration for production for the Georgia Institute of Technology Police Department.

N-4 has also won awards at the Georgia Institute of Technology, Industrial Design Launchpad Spring 2022 showcase for being the Best Project, and made it to the Final Round in IDSA IDEA Contest.
